Selected Projects

A careful selection of work that shows our range — conservation, new build, and adaptive reuse.

The River House

An adaptive reuse residence sited on a riverside plot. Long terraces, cross-ventilation and reused timber characterise the design.

Civic Loom

A small civic hub that stitched together low-cost construction with generous public space. Passive cooling and timber screens are key features.

Clay Studio

A maker's studio and gallery with high daylight, thermal mass walls, and flexible spaces for workshops and exhibitions.

The Meadow Pavilion

A timber-framed public structure set within a restored meadow landscape. The pavilion hosts seasonal events and demonstrates carbon-negative construction methods.

Urban Courtyard House

Inserted into a dense urban fabric, this home organizes around a planted inner courtyard that brings daylight, air, and privacy to all rooms. Brickwork detailing recalls the site's industrial heritage.

The Glass Workshop

Designed for an artist collective, this building balances diffused daylight and precise environmental control. Its north-facing sawtooth roof recalls historic factories while optimizing energy performance.

Harbor Learning Center

A civic building at the waterfront combining classrooms, exhibition space, and open terraces. The building’s layered façade mitigates wind and frames sea views for visitors and students alike.

Stone Ridge Chapel

Reinterpreting vernacular stone architecture, this small chapel uses local masonry and daylight modulation to create a serene, contemplative interior experience.
